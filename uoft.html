Winning Entry in the 2013 University of Toronto Institute for Sustainable Energy Banner Design Competition, which was a campus-wide event. Currently on display in the University of Toronto Mechanical & Industrial Engineering Energy Lab for 5 years. May also be reproduced on building facades, newsletters, magazines etc. This was my first major design award and the first big affirmation that I have the design skill set to succeed in the the future.

 

The Department of Mechanical & Industrial Engineering was looking for a design that can balance U of T's identity with an emphasis on sustainable energy.

 

This design solved the problem by adhering to the university's brand identity by using the official school colours and logo.

 

Wind turbines are common symbols of sustainable energy, and are placed in the foreground. The most well-received feature by the department and the judging committee was the background: a network of solar panels in U of T's official gray colour that also represents a power grid, and the flowing wires that plug into the Sun representing the energy harnessed from both solar and wind.
