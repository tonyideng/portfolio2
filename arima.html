The past year, I co-founded Adae, a start up that focused on a cashless online to offline marketplace. It was a great learning process, where I took on many roles such as designer, product developer, front-end web developer.

 

Below you will see the product we are working on and will launch in mid-February, 2016. It is a secure P2P marketplace where you can rent, sell, buy, lease, or offer your time (tutoring, helping out etc.). We are trying to solve the problem that many people encounter when using online classifieds sites that involve cash payments. We hope this product will let people have a peace of mind with their online to offline transactions.

This is a typical item page, here, the user has chosen to lease her item, this means the person leasing from her can opt to buy the item after the rental period.

This is the page where you can see all the listings posted by people, you can also post shout-outs to ask if anyone has something you want, and is not on the listings.

This is the Shout Outs page, where users can post a request for something they'd like to have. This mock up is displaying some Shout Outs where users want to rent something.

Our current idea of secure transactions to prevent scamming from buyers and sellers when they meet up in person revolves around our

Companion App, in which the buyer scans a unique Transaction QR code generated in the app. Only when the QR code is scanned, does the money transfer to the seller. For the person doing the scanning, they will see a QR scanner instead of a QR code.

This was the previous Q & A data visualization product I worked on with Arima, it grew initially but ultimately we moved on to what you saw before this. The development of this product nevertheless taught us many things .

Currently, we are deploying a revamped landing page to better let first time users know about our product and gain more users. This include a concise one pager, a pleasing background of cities that represent the "rest of the planet" since most users will be from cities, and a possible interactive widget so the user can quickly start using our product as a guest.

One of my early contributions was the redesign of our "Getting Started" process, after discussing with fellow team mates, we decided to combine both the sign-up process and getting started. The most important part of this page is making sure the sign up process is designed with a sense of assurance, conciseness, and safety, so that first time users are willing to further use our product.

This was the first iteration of the question and answer interface. The questions did not seem click-able, and users complained that it was dull and unfriendly.

I designed a new interface that had better typographic layout of the questions, and also cleaned up the screen space. The wording was also made to appear more personable for the user, and a filter for "Most Popular" and "Most Recent" was added. A Category side bar remains open by default for users to quickly jump across categories.

This future deployment will also feature contextual hovers, with green indicating an unanswered questions, and gray indicating an answered question.

Another feature will be a categories page that will display the featured question and the most popular question from each category, this page is is intended to act like a "home feed" for users to explore other parts the site that interests them.
