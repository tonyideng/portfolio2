BattleMaze is a game that I am currently working on with two of my developer friends. We were chatting one day, and wondered if we can combine the guessing aspect of BattleShip with the puzzle solving nature of a maze, and this is the result. We are constantly working together to improve the UX and UI, and are confident BattleMaze will be really fun once completed!

Player 1 can first construct walls anywhere by touching the grid lines to block Player 2's path.

On Player 2's turn, the screen clears and Player 1's walls become hidden. Player 2 must now guess his way to the goal.

Player 2 must keep his finger on the screen to navigate. If he runs into a wall (pictured) the path will disappear and he will need to start over. Player 1 will be able to see Player 2's path, and vice versa.

In the succeeding round on Player 2's turn to defend, the walls and goal turn red, this is a play on the classic Red vs Blue theme in games.

Player 1 navigates to the goal and succeeds. Notice Player 1's path is blue to distinguish from Player 2.

A quick drop down menu allows the player to quit at anytime, change settings, or create a new game.

Essentially, BattleMaze is a mind game that revolves around some guess work, memorization and luck paired with smart offense + defense.
